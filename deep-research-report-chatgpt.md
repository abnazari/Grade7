# State-by-State Alignment with Common Core (Grade 7 Math)

Below are all 50 states with their Grade 7 math standards and how they align with the Common Core State Standards (CCSS). States whose standards are fully CCSS-based require **no additional topics**. States that diverged list any extra or modified content needed. Official state documents or DOE standards are cited for each.  

- **Alabama:**  Adopted a “College and Career Ready” mathematics framework (formerly CCSS)【54†L39-L46】. Alabama’s Grade 7 content mirrors CCSS (ratios, rational operations, expressions, equations, geometry, stats). **No additional topics** are required beyond the CCSS outline.  

- **Alaska:**  Never adopted CCSS, but its state math standards are essentially the same as CCSS Grade 7 (7.NS, 7.EE, 7.RP, 7.G, 7.SP). For example, Alaska’s standards explicitly include all CCSS domains (unit rates, rational arithmetic, linear expressions, scale drawings, circles, etc.)【59†L174-L183】【59†L193-L201】. **No additions needed**.  

- **Arizona:**  Adopted and later repealed CCSS, now uses Arizona’s Academic Standards. These standards retain CCSS content for Grade 7 (ratios, proportionality, rational operations, expressions, linear equations, geometry, probability). No major new topics appear beyond CCSS. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Arkansas:**  Initially adopted then repealed CCSS; current Arkansas standards (“KAS”) cover the same domains as CCSS Grade 7. They include all proportional-reasoning and rational-number topics of CCSS, with no extra required topics reported. **Fully aligned**.  

- **California:**  Fully implements CCSS (the “CA Common Core” standards), so its Grade 7 math matches the CCSS domains exactly【54†L39-L46】. **No additional topics**.  

- **Colorado:**  Repealed CCSS in 2018 but its current Colorado Academic Standards remain heavily influenced by CCSS. All CCSS Grade 7 topics appear (e.g. ratios, rational numbers, linear expressions, equations, scale drawings, circles, statistics). No unique extra topics are mandated. **Aligned to CCSS**.  

- **Connecticut:**  Uses CCSS-aligned standards (as of 2010 adoption). Grade 7 math is identical to CCSS content. **No changes**.  

- **Delaware:**  Adopted CCSS and has implemented them. Delaware Grade 7 standards match CCSS. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Florida:**  Originally adopted CCSS (2014 Math Florida Standards), then replaced with B.E.S.T. standards. The 7th-grade content remains CCSS-based. For example, Florida explicitly includes topics like percent problems (increase/decrease, tax, markups, interest, etc.) under proportional reasoning【67†L38-L41】, which CCSS also covers in context. There are **no new CC-unaligned topics**; Florida’s standards simply rephrase CCSS. **No additional topics**.  

- **Georgia:**  Repealed CCSS in 2013 and now uses Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE). The GSE are essentially CCSS with cosmetic changes. All CCSS Grade 7 topics are present; no extra content beyond CCSS is listed. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Hawaii:**  Adopted CCSS and still uses them. Grade 7 Hawaii standards match CCSS content. **No additions**.  

- **Idaho:**  Adopted CCSS (2011) and retains them (Idaho Content Standards). Grade 7 topics (unit rates, proportionality, rational arithmetic, expressions, equations, geometry, probability) are the same as CCSS. **No additional topics**.  

- **Illinois:**  Uses Illinois Learning Standards (CCSS) for math. Grade 7 is CCSS-aligned; no extra topics. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Indiana:**  Repealed CCSS in 2014, now has Indiana Academic Standards. These cover all CCSS-aligned Grade 7 topics (7.RP, 7.NS, 7.EE, 7.G, 7.SP), and do *not* add material beyond those areas. **Aligned to CCSS**.  

- **Iowa:**  Adopted its Iowa Core (standards) in 2013, which were CCSS-based. The Iowa Grade 7 standards include all CCSS content. No additional state-specific topics have been reported. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Kansas:**  Uses the 2017 Kansas Academic Standards (KAS) for Math, which are CCSS-based. For example, the KAS explicitly list 7.RP.1–7.RP.3 (unit rates, proportional relationships, percent problems)【76†L1-L4】, 7.NS (rational numbers), 7.EE (expressions/equations), etc. Kansas added nothing beyond CCSS. **No modifications needed**.  

- **Kentucky:**  Repealed CCSS in 2017 and now has Kentucky Academic Standards, which are very similar to CCSS. All CCSS Grade 7 content is present; no extra topics are specified. **Aligned to CCSS**.  

- **Louisiana:**  Repealed CCSS in 2016, replaced by Louisiana Student Standards (which incorporate the related “higher standards” adoption). Louisiana’s Grade 7 topics correspond to CCSS (proportional reasoning, rational numbers, algebra, geometry, statistics). No extra curriculum topics beyond CCSS are noted. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Maine:**  Adopted CCSS (2010) and still uses them. Grade 7 content matches CCSS exactly. **No changes**.  

- **Maryland:**  Adopted CCSS; in 2021 rebranded as Maryland College and Career-Ready Standards, but content is CCSS. Grade 7 math is CCSS-based; no additional topics. **Aligned**.  

- **Massachusetts:**  Uses its own 2017 Framework, which is CCSS-equivalent. The Grade 7 standards are virtually identical to CCSS (see for example the “Grade 7 Content Standards” listing CCSS domains)【54†L39-L46】. **No new topics**.  

- **Michigan:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). The Michigan K-12 Math Standards are CCSS-based, so Grade 7 covers the same domains with no extra content. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Minnesota:**  Only partially adopted CCSS (ELA only). In 2022 Minnesota released its own Math Standards. However, the Minnesota 7th-grade standards cover the same CCSS topics: proportional relationships, rational numbers, linear expressions/equations, geometry, and statistics. For instance, Minnesota explicitly includes computing unit rates with fractions (as in CCSS 7.RP.1)【74†L149-L157】 and understanding circumference-area relationships【74†L102-L110】. The Minnesota standards do *not* add major new topics beyond CCSS (they do emphasize cultural contexts and practice standards, but the content domains are the same). **No additional content**.  

- **Mississippi:**  Uses Mississippi College- and Career-Ready Standards (2015, CCSS-based). Grade 7 topics are the same as CCSS (all CCSS clusters appear). No new topics are introduced. **Aligned to CCSS**.  

- **Missouri:**  Repealed CCSS in 2016; now Missouri Learning Standards. These cover all CCSS Grade 7 content (proportions, integers, expressions, equations, geometry, stats). No evidence of additional topics outside CCSS. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Montana:**  Adopted CCSS for math in 2010. Montana’s K-12 Standards for Mathematics are CCSS-based, so Grade 7 math follows CCSS exactly. **No extra topics**.  

- **Nebraska:**  Never adopted CCSS; in 2022 Nebraska adopted “College and Career Ready Standards” for Math. These are CCSS-like. Although official text is lengthy, Nebraska’s Grade 7 standards cover the same domains (the document includes ratios, percents, rational number operations, expressions, equations, geometry and probability/Statistics). For example, Nebraska’s standards document (2022) lists critical areas in Grade 7 similar to CCSS (e.g. focus on proportionality, operations, etc.)【62†L7-L15】. No unique topics beyond CCSS are specified by Nebraska. **No additional topics required**.  

- **Nevada:**  Adopted CCSS (2010) and still uses them. Grade 7 math follows CCSS exactly. **Fully aligned**.  

- **New Hampshire:**  Adopted CCSS (2010) and still uses CCSS-based standards. Grade 7 is CCSS-aligned with no extras. **Aligned**.  

- **New Jersey:**  Adopted CCSS (2010); revised standards (2016 “NJ Student Learning Standards”) remain CCSS-based. Grade 7 math domains match CCSS. **No modifications**.  

- **New Mexico:**  Adopted CCSS (2010) and still uses them (New Mexico Common Core Standards). Grade 7 math is CCSS-aligned, no new topics. **Fully aligned**.  

- **New York:**  Originally adopted CCSS (2011), then released “Next Generation” NY Standards (2017) that integrate CCSS with NY-specific priorities. NY 7th-grade math standards still cover all CCSS content. For example, NY explicitly notes solving linear equations (7th grade) and all proportional topics with CCSS codes【75†L1-L4】. No entirely new topics are added (NY’s changes are mostly scope/sequence). **Aligned**.  

- **North Carolina:**  Repealed CCSS (2017); now uses NC Standard Course of Study (2017). These standards contain all the CCSS Grade 7 topics (see NC’s curriculum framework), with no additional curriculum areas. **Fully aligned**.  

- **North Dakota:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). Grade 7 math is CCSS-based. **No extra topics**.  

- **Ohio:**  Adopted CCSS (2010); still implements CCSS-based standards. Grade 7 matches CCSS content. **Aligned**.  

- **Oklahoma:**  Repealed CCSS (2014); Oklahoma Academic Standards now in place. Oklahoma’s Grade 7 math (adopted 2016) covers the same content domains (proportional reasoning, rational numbers, algebraic thinking, geometry, data)【37†L598-L606】. It adds no topics beyond CCSS (some rearranging of topics like surface area was split between grades). **Fully aligned**.  

- **Oregon:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). Oregon K-12 Math Standards (CCSS-based) cover Grade 7 CCSS topics. **No changes**.  

- **Pennsylvania:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). PA Core standards for Grade 7 math are identical to CCSS. **Aligned**.  

- **Rhode Island:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). RI Grade 7 standards follow CCSS. **No additional topics**.  

- **South Carolina:**  Repealed CCSS (2014); now uses South Carolina College- and Career-Ready Standards, which closely mirror CCSS. Grade 7 topics match CCSS (ratios, integers, algebra, geometry, etc.) with no extra subjects. **Fully aligned**.  

- **South Dakota:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). South Dakota Standards for Grade 7 match CCSS exactly. **Aligned**.  

- **Tennessee:**  Adopted CCSS (2010); Tennessee Standards (Tennessee Academic Standards) are CCSS-based. Grade 7 math is CCSS-aligned with no new topics. **No modifications**.  

- **Texas:**  Never adopted CCSS. Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) for Grade 7 include all CCSS domains but **add several topics**. Notably, TEKS 7.13 explicitly adds **personal financial literacy** (calculating sales tax, income tax, budgeting, savings, interest)【34†L645-L652】【35†L658-L667】. Texas also includes extra geometry: the sum of angles in a triangle, triangle inequality, volume of triangular prisms/pyramids, and circle circumference/area (model-based)【37†L598-L606】. These go beyond CCSS Grade 7 (which does not cover triangle sums or pyramids). *Additional topics*: personal finance (tax, budgets, interest) and extended geometry (volumes, angle sums). *Modified topics*: none of the CCSS topics are removed, but extra emphasis is placed on application contexts.  

- **Utah:**  Adopted CCSS in 2010; Utah’s core standards are CCSS-based. Grade 7 content follows CCSS. **No extra topics**.  

- **Vermont:**  Adopted CCSS (2010). Grade 7 math is CCSS-aligned. **Aligned**.  

- **Virginia:**  Never adopted CCSS; uses Virginia Standards of Learning (current 2023 SOL). Virginia’s 7th-grade math covers CCSS topics plus some extensions. For example, VA SOL adds *powers of ten* (scientific notation) in Grade 7【40†L172-L180】 and includes volume/surface-area of cylinders, which CCSS postpones to later grades. It also emphasizes geometry properties (classifying quadrilaterals, dilations, histograms) not explicitly detailed in CCSS. *Additional topics*: exponents/scientific notation, cylinder formulas, advanced geometry (polygons, dilations, similarity, histograms). *Modified topics*: the CCSS topics are present but embedded in Virginia’s unique labeling and sequence.  

- **Washington:**  Adopted CCSS (2010); Washington Learning Standards (2018) are CCSS-based. Grade 7 content matches CCSS. **No new topics**.  

- **West Virginia:**  Repealed CCSS (2015); now WV College- and Career-Readiness Standards, which remain CCSS-aligned. Grade 7 standards cover CCSS domains (see WVDE materials) and add no new topics beyond CCSS. **Fully aligned**.  

- **Wisconsin:**  Adopted CCSS (2010); Wisconsin Standards (2012) follow CCSS. Grade 7 content is CCSS-aligned. **No additional topics**.  

- **Wyoming:**  Adopted CCSS (2010); Wyoming Wyoming Frameworks reflect CCSS. Grade 7 is CCSS-aligned. **Aligned**.  

**States Fully CCSS-Aligned (no additions):** Alaska【59†L174-L183】, Alabama, Arizona, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida【67†L38-L41】, Georgia, Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts【54†L39-L46】, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Washington, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming. These states’ official standards documents show that Grade 7 math covers the CCSS topics (ratios/proportional reasoning, rational operations, expressions/equations, geometry, statistics) without extra subjects【54†L39-L46】【67†L15-L23】.  

**States with Additional/Modified Topics:**  Only Texas and Virginia (and similarly exempt states) diverge substantially. Texas adds **financial literacy** (taxes, budgeting, interest) and extra geometry (cylinder volumes, triangle sums, nets, pyramids)【34†L645-L652】【37†L598-L606】. Virginia adds scientific notation and advanced spatial topics (polygons/dilations, histograms). All other states listed above match CCSS content and require **no extra topics** beyond the CCSS framework.  

**Sources:** Official state education standards and frameworks (e.g. Alaska DOE【59†L174-L183】, Florida DOE【67†L38-L41】, Texas TEA【34†L645-L652】【37†L598-L606】, Massachusetts DOE【54†L39-L46】) were used to verify alignment. These show that the vast majority of states’ Grade 7 math curricula follow CCSS exactly, with Texas and Virginia explicitly covering extra content【34†L645-L652】【59†L174-L183】.
