## Baseline: Grade 7 Common Core

The official Grade 7 Common Core State Standards for Mathematics (CCSS‑M) organize content in five domains: Ratios \& Proportional Relationships, The Number System, Expressions \& Equations, Geometry, and Statistics \& Probability. Key grade‑7 expectations include:[^5][^3][^4]

- Proportional relationships, unit rates with fractions, and percent problems including discounts, markups, taxes, tips, percent increase/decrease, and simple interest.[^3][^4]
- Operations with signed rational numbers (all four operations, including with complex fractions) and solving multistep real‑world problems.[^4][^3]
- Writing, simplifying, and rewriting expressions; solving two‑step equations and inequalities of the forms $px+q=r$ and $p(x+q)=r$.[^6][^3]
- Scale drawings, basic constructions, triangle conditions, circle circumference and area, composite area, surface area of prisms, and volume of prisms.[^3]
- Random sampling, comparing two populations, and informal probability and probability models, including compound events and simulations.[^6][^3]

## How to use this for your book

- **States effectively “based on Common Core” in grade‑7 math**
From a content perspective, *all* states except Texas and Minnesota are using CCSS‑M directly or a very close derivative for grade‑7 math. This includes states listed in adoption summaries as “Adopted” or “Adopted with changes,” and even Alaska and Nebraska, whose current math standards and grade‑7 excerpts clearly mirror CCSS domains and exemplars.[^12][^1][^2][^9][^4][^3]
- **States needing substantive *additional* topics vs your current outline**
    - **Texas (TEKS)** – add explicit personal financial literacy topics: income/payroll tax, net worth, budgeting, and compound interest; make sure there are plenty of multi‑step percent/finance word problems.[^34][^35][^36]
    - **Minnesota** – consider adding a short, explicit “functions and patterns” section (inputs/outputs, function notation, patterns in tables and graphs) inside your Expressions/Equations chapter to match Minnesota’s “Patterns \& Relationships” emphasis.[^10]
- **States needing only *modified/emphasized* topics**
For Florida (B.E.S.T.), Virginia (SOL), Nebraska (CCR), Indiana/Missouri/South Carolina/Oklahoma/Tennessee/New Jersey/Massachusetts, and similar “replacement” states, your existing chapters already cover the required grade‑7 mathematics. What you’ll mainly need for the state editions is:
    - different **labels and crosswalk tables** (state codes ↔ CCSS codes),
    - some shifts in **which examples you foreground** (e.g., Virginia’s process goals; Florida’s MTRs; Nebraska’s “Ratios \& Proportions” strand),
    - and, in a few cases, marking your more advanced probability simulations as **optional/enrichment** if a state keeps probability lighter at grade 7.[^18][^19][^39][^40][^21][^25][^9]
