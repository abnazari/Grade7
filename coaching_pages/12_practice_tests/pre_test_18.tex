Here's a game-changing strategy: don't try to solve every question in order. Instead, go through the test twice:

\vspace{4mm}

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{|>{\centering\arraybackslash\bfseries\color{funOrangeDark}}p{2.5cm}|>{\centering\arraybackslash}p{4cm}|>{\centering\arraybackslash}p{3cm}|}
\hline
\rowcolor{funOrange!15} \textbf{Pass} & \textbf{What to Do} & \textbf{Questions} \\
\hline
\faForward~Pass 1 & Answer easy ones quickly & As many as you can \\
\hline
\faRedo~Pass 2 & Return to hard/skipped & The rest \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\vspace{3mm}

This way, you never waste time staring at a hard question while easy points wait on the next page.

\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{3mm}

Use this method on today's test. Circle any question you skip, so you can find it easily on Pass~2. Notice how much calmer you feel when you stop fighting hard questions.
\end{tcolorbox}
